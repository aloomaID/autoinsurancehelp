FAQ - Auto Insurance Services

Is it mandatory to have auto insurance in Pennsylvania Pa?

Yes, the state of Pennsylvania Pa requires all drivers to carry a minimum level of liability auto insurance coverage.

What is gap insurance, and do I need it in Pennsylvania Pa?

Gap insurance covers the difference between what you owe on your car loan and the actual value of your vehicle if it's totaled in Pennsylvania Pa. It’s useful for people who owe more than their car is worth.

What discounts are available for auto insurance in Pennsylvania Pa?

Auto Insurance offers discounts for safe drivers, multi-vehicle policies, good students, bundling policies, and more in Pennsylvania Pa.

How do I get an auto insurance quote in Pennsylvania Pa?

What is collision coverage in auto insurance?

Collision coverage helps pay for repairs or replacement of your car if it's damaged in an accident, regardless of who’s at fault.

How does comprehensive coverage work in Pennsylvania Pa?

Comprehensive coverage pays for damage to your vehicle not caused by a collision, such as theft, vandalism, or natural disasters in Pennsylvania Pa.

Is personal injury protection (PIP) required in Pennsylvania Pa?

Personal injury protection (PIP) is optional in Pennsylvania Pa, but it provides medical coverage for you and your passengers in the event of an accident.

Can I add roadside assistance to my auto insurance in Pennsylvania Pa?

Yes, roadside assistance can be added to your auto insurance policy in Pennsylvania Pa for services like towing, battery jumps, and tire changes.

What happens if I let my auto insurance lapse in Pennsylvania Pa?

Letting your auto insurance lapse in Pennsylvania Pa could result in fines, penalties, or loss of coverage. It’s important to maintain continuous coverage to stay compliant with the law.
